The main problems in education

Education faces significant challenges, as students are not typically taught effective study methods in schools or universities. Instead, they often rely on memorizing information for exams and practicing repetitive tasks, which does not result in genuine comprehension or long-term retention.

Conclusion: it is necessary to know how to learn, but for some reason, this is not taught in many places.

To address this issue, it is essential to develop specific techniques and exercises that can help students learn how to learn. By changing their approach to studying and making it more efficient and engaging, they can gain a deeper understanding of the material. The Practicum is an example of a program that utilizes these techniques to teach effective learning strategies.

Learning techniques.

Five practices from Barbara Oakley.

  • Two types of attention

    Deep knowledge arises when alternating between focused and diffuse thinking.

  • Recall

    Recalling learned material allows you to connect disparate portions of memory.

  • Interleaving

    Study multiple skills simultaneously, they enrich each other.

  • Questions

    While listening to the teacher, come up with a good question that really interests you.

  • Illusion of competence

    Repeat to yourself, write down, tell a friend: you only think that you have mastered the new topic.

Barbara Oakley's story

Barbara Oakley had a challenging relationship with mathematics from an early age and considered herself a complete humanitarian, lacking intelligence. However, during her time in the army, she studied Russian to earn extra pay, and her success in doing so led to her promotion to a commander. Despite her achievements, to advance in her career, Barbara needed to pass math exams, which presented a significant challenge. Undeterred, she devised her own approach to mastering the subject. Barbara realized that when something is "difficult" to learn, the knowledge gained through hard work is often much deeper than that gained through easy comprehension. Her approach helped her succeed, and she went on to become an expert in learning and the development of intelligence.

The learning principles:

by Josh Kaufman

  • 1

    Choose a compelling project.

  • 2

    Focus on one skill at a time.

  • 3

    Determine the target level of proficiency.

  • 4

    Break the skill down into manageable parts.

  • 5

    Prepare for practice.

  • 6

    Remove barriers to practice.

  • 7

    Set dedicated time for practice.

  • 8

    Create fast feedback loops.

  • 9

    Practice in short, intense sessions on a schedule.

  • 10

    Pay attention to quantity and speed.
